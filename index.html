Interesting facts about camel

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  • Thanks to thick pads of skin on their chest and knees, camels can comfortably sit in very hot sand.

Interesting facts about chameleon

  • They mainly change there colors in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.
  • Chameleon’s tongue is propelled by incredible speed: it takes 0.07 seconds for tongue to reach the victim
  • and birds are the most common predators of the chameleon, along with some mammals.

Interesting facts about Frog

  • Frogs don't need to drink water as they absorb it through their skin.
  • A frog's call is unique to its species, and some frog calls can be heard up to a mile away.
  • Some frogs can jump over 20 times their own body length; that is like a human jumping 30m.

Interesting facts about Crane

  • Cranes are powerful flyers, with some having the ability to fly over the Himalayas.
  • During migration cranes fly with their head and neck straight out and their feet and legs straight behind them.
  • Their migration is so long and hard that many die from fatigue, hunger, or predation from birds of prey.
